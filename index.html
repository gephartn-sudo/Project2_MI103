Writing about TTRPGs

I've played with a couple of different TTRPG systems - here are a few of them and my opinions on them, as well as a few facts about them. The links after the paragraphs contain information about individual characters (such as attributes, skills, etc.)

Dungeons & Dragons Fifth Edition

This is the first TTRPG that I played with, and I really like it. It feels beginner friendly, and is also the most popular of the three I'm writing about when it comes to people playing them, be it in person, online, or digital campaigns on YouTube. I currently am a part of several campaigns, in which I play as an undead fighter, an elf artificer, and a aasimar sorcerer.

Generic Universal Role Playing System (GURPS)

This is the second TTRPG system I've ever played. This one is far more complex, but the first variant of GURPS I played with had a subset of rules that were much more digestible. To illustrate my point, the base rules of GURPS 4th edition has about 600 pages, meanwhile the subset of GURPS rules I played with (GURPS Dungeon Fantasy) has about 120 in the main rule book. GURPS is FAR more customizable than D&D5e, however, and it uses 3d6 for nearly ALL rolls.
Learn more about Generic Universal Role Playing System (GURPS)

CAIN

This TTRPG system I only played a couple of sessions of as a one-shot campaign, however it still sunk its teeth into me. Its setting is akin to Chainsaw Man and Jujutsu Kaisen, and has roughly as much customizability as D&D5e I would argue. The dice rolling system is unique, however, as it's based off of how many successes on a d6 you make rather than a specific number that you have to meet or beat.
